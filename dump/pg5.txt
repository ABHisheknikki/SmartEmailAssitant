Page 5: Trading and Investment Policies

These policies apply to all employees who are involved in trading or investment decisions.

Personal Trading:

Employees are permitted to trade securities for their personal accounts, but they must do so in a manner that does not create conflicts of interest or violate insider trading laws.

Employees must pre-clear all personal trades with the Compliance department.

Employees are prohibited from trading in securities that are on the company's restricted list.

Employees must report all personal trades to the Compliance department on a quarterly basis.

Investment Recommendations:

Investment recommendations must be based on thorough research and analysis.

Investment recommendations must be objective and unbiased.

Investment recommendations must be suitable for the client's investment objectives and risk tolerance.

Employees must disclose any conflicts of interest that may affect their investment recommendations.

Order Execution:

Orders must be executed in a timely and efficient manner.

Orders must be executed at the best available price.

Orders must be allocated fairly among clients.

Portfolio Management:

Portfolios must be managed in accordance with the client's investment objectives and risk tolerance.

Portfolios must be diversified to reduce risk.

Portfolios must be monitored regularly to ensure that they are performing as expected.

Restrictions on Front-Running:

Front-running, the practice of trading securities based on advance knowledge of a pending block order, is strictly prohibited. Employees are prohibited from using information about pending client orders for their personal benefit.
